Makhana vs Popcorn vs Almonds: Which Healthy Snack Wins?

By The Makhana · Updated 22 June 2026 · 6 min read

In the makhana vs popcorn debate, makhana usually comes out ahead for everyday snacking: it carries more plant protein (around 9g per 100g), is naturally gluten-free, and is roasted in cold-pressed or olive oil rather than deep-fried or buttered. Plain popcorn is still a sound, fibre-rich choice, and almonds win on protein and healthy fats — but they are also the most calorie-dense of the three. The right pick really depends on what you want from that handful.

Quick answer

  • Makhana is the most balanced everyday snack — light, lower in fat and roasted, never fried.
  • Popcorn (air-popped or lightly roasted) is great for high-volume, low-calorie munching, but flavoured cinema versions can pile on oil and salt.
  • Almonds top the table for protein and healthy fats, yet they are the most calorie-dense of the three.
  • For a protein-and-crunch hit without heavy fat, makhana sits comfortably in the middle.

How do they compare per 100g?

Nutrition shifts with brand, oil and seasoning, so treat these as indicative ranges for plain, lightly roasted versions. They are a useful starting point for the makhana vs popcorn vs almonds comparison.

The headline takeaway: makhana is the lowest in fat by a wide margin, popcorn leads on fibre per 100g, and almonds dominate protein and energy. Remember that 100g of almonds is a large portion, while 100g of makhana is a generous, airy bowlful.

Calories and portion size

Calories per 100g only tell half the story, because makhana and popcorn are bulky and light, while almonds are compact and dense. A satisfying handful plays out very differently:

  • A typical 30g serving of makhana is roughly 105 kcal and fills a small bowl.
  • A 30g serving of almonds (about 23 nuts) is closer to 170 kcal.
  • Air-popped popcorn is the volume champion — lots of crunch for very few calories.

If you like to munch slowly through a big bowl while watching a film, makhana and popcorn give you more to eat for less energy. Almonds reward smaller, measured portions.

Which has the most protein and fibre?

Almonds are the clear protein leader at around 21g per 100g, with makhana offering a respectable ~9g of plant protein and popcorn landing in between. For fibre, popcorn (a whole grain) and almonds both score well, while makhana adds a useful, gentler amount. If your goal is maximum protein per bite, almonds win; if you want protein and a light, high-volume snack, makhana is the balanced pick. Fat, frying and how they're made

This is where makhana stands apart. Our makhana is roasted in cold-pressed or olive oil, never deep-fried, with no palm oil, no maida and no MSG. Almonds are naturally high in fat — mostly the heart-friendly monounsaturated kind — which is good in moderation but adds up quickly. Popcorn is low in fat when air-popped, yet many ready-to-eat and cinema-style packs are cooked in cheaper oils and finished with butter, sugar or heavy salt. Always read the label: the cooking method often matters more than the grain or seed itself.

Satiety, gluten and use-cases

All three are naturally gluten-free, which makes them friendly options for coeliac and gluten-sensitive snackers (always check for cross-contamination on the pack). On satiety, almonds keep you fullest thanks to protein and fat, makhana satisfies through volume and a slow, crunchy chew, and popcorn fills the bowl but can leave you reaching for more if it is lightly seasoned. Where each tends to shine:

  1. Makhana — desk snacking, evening tea-time, travel, and guilt-light film nights.
  2. Popcorn — big-bowl movie sessions where volume is the point.
  3. Almonds — a compact, energy-dense bite before a workout or a long commute.

So, which snack wins?

Each has a moment. Popcorn wins when you want maximum volume for minimum calories and you keep it plain. Almonds win when you need concentrated protein and healthy fats in a small, portable portion. And makhana wins as the balanced, everyday choice — light, roasted not fried, naturally gluten-free, with useful plant protein and a satisfying crunch you can enjoy by the bowl. For most people, most of the time, that middle path is exactly what an everyday snack should be.

Frequently asked questions

Is makhana healthier than popcorn?

For most everyday snacking, makhana edges ahead of popcorn. It offers more plant protein (around 9g per 100g), is naturally gluten-free and is roasted in cold-pressed or olive oil rather than deep-fried or coated in butter. Plain air-popped popcorn is also a good choice, but flavoured cinema-style popcorn can be high in oil, salt and sugar.

Which has more protein, makhana or almonds?

Almonds are higher in protein, with roughly 21g per 100g compared to about 9g per 100g in makhana. However, almonds are also far higher in fat and calories. Makhana gives you a lighter, lower-fat snack while still adding useful plant protein to your day.

Is makhana good for weight loss?

Makhana can fit well into a weight-management plan because it is light, low in fat and satisfying to munch on in larger volumes for relatively few calories. Portion size still matters, especially with flavoured varieties. This is general information and not medical advice; speak to a qualified professional for a plan suited to you.

Can diabetics eat makhana?

Makhana is often chosen as a snack by people managing blood sugar because it is roasted, not fried, and provides fibre and protein. Everyone responds differently, so this is general information rather than medical advice. If you have diabetes, please confirm portion sizes and frequency with your doctor or dietitian.
