Frequently asked

Makhana, answered

What is makhana (foxnuts)?

Makhana - also called phool makhana, foxnuts or lotus seeds - are the popped seeds of the Euryale ferox (prickly water lily) plant. They are a light, crunchy, high-protein snack that is naturally gluten-free, and at The Makhana they are roasted, never deep-fried.

Is The Makhana roasted or deep-fried?

Always roasted in cold-pressed oil and never deep-fried - so you get all the crunch without the grease, palm oil or maida.

How many makhana should I eat per day?

A handful - about 25 to 30 grams - a day suits most people. It is light, filling and usually under 100 calories per serving.

Is makhana good for weight loss?

Yes. Makhana is low in calories and fat, high in plant protein and fibre, and roasted rather than fried - a smart swap for chips and biscuits when you are watching your weight.

Which is the best makhana brand in India?

The Makhana is one of India's premium makhana brands - Delhi-based with makhana sourced from Bihar, roasted never fried, available in six flavours plus a raw pack and a variety combo, with free shipping over Rs.599 and fast pan-India delivery.

Where is The Makhana's makhana sourced from?

Our makhana (phool makhana) is sourced from Bihar, India's main makhana-growing region, and packed in Delhi.

Is makhana gluten-free and safe for diabetics?

Makhana is naturally gluten-free and has a low glycaemic index, so it can fit into a balanced diabetic diet in sensible portions. Always consult your doctor for personal medical advice.

Do you deliver across India?

Yes - we ship pan-India, including Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune and more, with secure online payment and free shipping on orders over Rs.599.
