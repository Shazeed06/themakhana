10 Easy Makhana Recipes: From Roasted Snacks to Kheer

By The Makhana · Updated 22 June 2026 · 7 min read

The easiest makhana recipes start with crisp, roasted foxnuts and a quick seasoning — think roasted masala makhana, peri peri makhana or a tangy makhana chaat that come together in minutes. Once you have crunchy puffs ready, the same humble seed can turn into a creamy kheer, a cooling raita or even a comforting curry. Below are 10 simple recipes that take makhana from everyday snack to festive dessert.

Quick answer

  • Always roast raw makhana first (6–8 minutes on low heat) until crisp before seasoning.
  • Use a little cold-pressed or olive oil and your favourite spices — no deep-frying needed.
  • Savoury snacks (masala, peri peri, chaat, namkeen mix) take under 15 minutes.
  • The same makhana works in desserts (kheer, caramel) and meals (raita, curry, bhel).
  • Start with the variety combo if you want flavours ready to cook with.

How do you roast makhana for recipes?

Roasting is the foundation of almost every makhana recipe. Heat a heavy pan on low, add raw phool makhana (with or without a teaspoon of oil) and stir constantly for 6 to 8 minutes until the puffs turn light, crisp and snap cleanly when pressed. Cool fully before seasoning or storing so they stay crunchy.

1. Roasted Masala Makhana

The everyday classic. Roast 2 cups makhana in 1 tsp cold-pressed oil until crisp. Sprinkle ½ tsp roasted cumin powder, ¼ tsp red chilli, a pinch of black salt and chaat masala. Toss for a minute on low heat and serve warm. A perfect tea-time handful.

2. Peri Peri Makhana

For a fiery kick, roast 2 cups makhana, then toss with ½ tsp peri peri seasoning, a pinch of paprika, salt and a few drops of oil to help it cling. Roast 1 more minute so the spice toasts. This is the same bold flavour as our Peri Peri Punch — great for movie nights.

3. Makhana Chaat

A tangy, fresh snack. Mix 2 cups roasted makhana with chopped onion, tomato, boiled potato and coriander. Add a squeeze of lemon, ½ tsp chaat masala, green chutney and a little sev. Assemble just before eating so the makhana stays crisp.

Can makhana be made into a dessert?

Absolutely — foxnuts soften beautifully in milk, which makes them ideal for Indian sweets. Their mild flavour soaks up cardamom, saffron and jaggery without overpowering them.

4. Makhana Kheer

Lightly roast 1 cup makhana, then crush half of it coarsely. Simmer in 500 ml milk with 3 tbsp sugar (or jaggery), ¼ tsp cardamom and a few saffron strands until creamy, about 15 minutes. Garnish with chopped almonds and pistachios. A festive favourite.

5. Caramel Makhana

For a sweet treat, melt 3 tbsp jaggery or sugar with 1 tsp ghee until it bubbles, then quickly toss in 2 cups roasted makhana so each puff is coated. Spread on a greased tray to set and break into clusters. Enjoy in moderation, as this adds sugar.

What savoury makhana mixes can I make?

Makhana is a brilliant base for trail mixes and namkeen because it stays light and carries spice well. Build these once and snack through the week.

6. Makhana Namkeen Mix

Roast 2 cups makhana, then toss with roasted peanuts, curry leaves, ¼ tsp turmeric, hing, salt and red chilli. A teaspoon of oil bloomed with the spices ties it together. A lighter take on classic namkeen.

7. Makhana Trail Mix

Combine 1 cup roasted makhana with almonds, walnuts, pumpkin seeds and a handful of raisins or dried cranberries. Keep it unsweetened for a high-energy, on-the-go snack. No cooking needed if your makhana is already roasted.

8. Makhana Bhel

A fun twist on bhel puri. Toss 2 cups roasted makhana with puffed rice, chopped onion, tomato, raw mango, tamarind and green chutney, and coriander. Top with sev and lemon. Mix and serve immediately for maximum crunch.

Makhana in main meals: raita and curry

Beyond snacks and sweets, makhana adds texture to everyday meals — a crunchy lift to raita and a hearty bite to curries.

9. Makhana Raita

Whisk 1 cup curd with roasted cumin, black salt and a pinch of chilli. Fold in ½ cup lightly crushed roasted makhana just before serving so it keeps a gentle bite. A cooling side for biryani or parathas.

10. Makhana Curry (Makhana Matar)

Sauté onion, ginger and garlic, add tomato puree and spices, then a splash of cream or cashew paste for a rich gravy. Stir in 1½ cups roasted makhana and ½ cup green peas, simmer 5 minutes until the puffs soften slightly. Serve with roti or rice for a satisfying vegetarian curry.

Tips to keep your makhana recipes crisp

  • Roast on low heat and stir constantly — high heat scorches before the centre crisps.
  • Add oil sparingly; makhana needs only a touch to carry spice.
  • For chaat, bhel and raita, add wet ingredients last.
  • Store cooled makhana in an airtight container away from moisture.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to roast raw makhana before using it in recipes?

Yes. Raw phool makhana tastes bland and slightly soft, so dry-roast it on low heat for 6 to 8 minutes (or in a little oil) until each puff is crisp. This step is essential for snack recipes. If you start with our ready-to-eat roasted makhana, you can skip roasting and go straight to seasoning or assembling.

Are makhana recipes healthy?

Makhana is naturally gluten-free, non-GMO and a good source of plant protein, with roughly 9g of protein per 100g. Dry-roasted or lightly oil-roasted recipes keep it light, while caramel or kheer versions add sugar and are best enjoyed in moderation. This is general information and not medical advice.

How do I keep roasted makhana crunchy?

Cool the roasted makhana completely before storing, then keep it in an airtight container away from moisture. Add wet ingredients like chutney or yoghurt only just before serving so the puffs stay crisp instead of turning soft.

Can children eat makhana snacks?

Makhana makes a light, low-spice snack that many families offer to older children, ideally lightly seasoned and served under supervision because the puffs can be a choking risk for very young kids. This is general information and not medical advice — check with your paediatrician for your child.
